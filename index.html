About

Currently I am the Digital A/V Lab Technician for the Telecommunications Department at Ball State University. Oversee, manage and maintain various Pro Tools audio editing and Final Cut video editing installations used by Telecommunications and other students; serve as the first contact for all faculty and student question and/or problems with any equipment in assigned areas. I planned out at $250,000 budget for audio equipment upgrades and installed it. I wrote several tutorials for faculty and students.

I have gotten involved with many student projects to keep up sound skills sharp. A couple of years ago I worked on The Blue, I was brought on in Post Production. Dyscrasia was a large scale student film, 50+ production crew. Two films are currently in Pre Production with anther in the planning stages. I am doing Sound Design and Foley Mixing on an animation called Sea-tectives. Happy Snow Day Franny was a non studendt short film that I worked on the Sound Design.

I graduated from Ball State University with a degree in Telecommunications; focus on audio production. In my last couple of years I started working in video production and web design. I recorded Foley and voice overs, with the history department, for a game about the Underground Railroad. I also had an internship at WIPB, local Muncie PBS affiliate, where I worked with the engineering staff.

My strongest software skills are in Pro Tools, Premiere, Audition, After Effects, and Photoshop. I also have experience with Final Cut Pro and Motion. I have worked with Zoom H4n, Sound Devices 702 and Fostex FR-2. I have in studio experience with Avid D-Command, D-Control and C-24

Band Recordings

I recorded the Apathy Wizards at the Letterman Studios at Ball State University. The recording took a week and was filled with lots of experiments. This was the first time that both studios were joined together for one recording session. It was also my first experience at recording a double bass, djembe, flute and mandolin. Most of the instruments had multiple microphones, so that I could mix them together and get the best sound. I also worked with several room mics and created my own reverb by changing the setup of the studio.


Glorious Rebellion came to the studios to record some live in studio music videos. After they were done with the video shot, they decided they wanted to do an acoustic version of a song off one of their EPs. I experimented with various room mics to get the background vocals.


Vinyl Jackets is one of the bands that I recorded while in the summer music immersion class. I worked with a group of 6 people over a weekend. The mixing was done individually.


Cecil is one of the bands that I recorded while in the summer music immersion class. I worked with a team of 4 people and recorded them over 3 days. The mixing was done individually.
