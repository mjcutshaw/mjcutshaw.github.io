About

Currently my days are filled working at Ball State Univerity (BSU), as the Digital A/V Lab Technician. My nights and weekends, however, are packed with filmmaking, learning new software, playing music, and, when time allows, game nights with friends (when none of us are filming).

At BSU, my job duties include, but are not limited to, overseeing, managing, and maintaining a wide range of Pro Tools and Final Cut editing installations used by Telecommunications students, staff, and faculty. I serve as the first contact for all students, staff, and faculty in regards to questions and/or problems with the equipment/labs in assigned areas. I have constructed a $250,000 audio equipment budget for the Telecommunication department. This budget, as well as others I am frequently involved with, does not stop there as I ensure upgrades are properly installed.

Band Recordings

I recorded the Apathy Wizards at the Letterman Studios at Ball State University. The recording took a week and was filled with lots of experiments. This was the first time that both studios were joined together for one recording session. It was also my first experience at recording a double bass, djembe, flute and mandolin. Most of the instruments had multiple microphones, so that I could mix them together and get the best sound. I also worked with several room mics and created my own reverb by changing the setup of the studio.


Glorious Rebellion came to the studios to record some live in studio music videos. After they were done with the video shot, they decided they wanted to do an acoustic version of a song off one of their EPs. I experimented with various room mics to get the background vocals.

  • Client: Class Work
  • Date: 2009-2012
  • Role: Audio Engineer

Caring Companions

Volunteer work was provided for Franciscan Health in Indianapolis, IN for a program called Memories to Hold. Video was shot and then edited for the program to submit to the Drew Brees Foundation and the program was awarded $1500 to help with future Walks.
